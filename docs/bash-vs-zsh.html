Bash's four modes

Bash behaves differently depending on two independent switches: login vs non-login, and interactive vs non-interactive. Which config files get sourced depends on where you land in this grid.

💡 bash is the safe assumption for scripts — it's everywhere, from containers to servers. zsh shines as your interactive daily driver — completion, correction, and plugins make typing commands nicer, but don't assume it exists on a remote box. Most people write #!/bin/bash at the top of scripts and use zsh (often via Oh My Zsh) in their own terminal.
